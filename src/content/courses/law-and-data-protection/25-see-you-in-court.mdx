## Two Types of Judicial Remedies

### 1. Suing the Supervisory Authority (Article 78)
If the SA ignores a complaint, or if the SA makes a decision that the user (or Bob!) hates, they can take the **SA to court**.
*   This usually happens in the Member State where the SA is established.

### 2. Suing the Controller or Processor (Article 79)
Users can sue Bob (the Controller) or his cloud provider (the Processor) directly for violating their rights.
*   **Where?** In the Member State where the company has an establishment, OR where the data subject lives.
*   **Exception:** If the controller is a **public authority** (like a government tax office), you usually must sue them in their own state's courts.

## The Role of the CJEU
Sometimes, a national judge isn't sure how to interpret the GDPR. They ask the **Court of Justice of the European Union (CJEU)** for help. This is called a **Preliminary Ruling**.

The CJEU doesn't decide if Bob is guilty. It just explains what the Law means. The national judge then takes that explanation and applies it to Bob's case.
