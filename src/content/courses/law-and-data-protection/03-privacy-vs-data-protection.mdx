# The "Creepy" Feature

Bob's app is growing. He decides to add a "Stalker Mode" (he calls it "Friend Tracker") that logs every user's location every 5 minutes to "improve the algorithm." He proudly shows it to Alice.

Alice recoils. "Bob, that's creepy. You can't just track people like that."

"But it's secure!" Bob argues. "I used AES-256 encryption. Nobody can steal the data. It's protected!"

Alice sighs. "Bob, you're confusing **Data Protection** (Security) with **Data Privacy**."

## Privacy vs. Data Protection (Security)

These two terms are often used interchangeably, but they mean very different things in the legal world.

### 1. Data Protection (Security)
This is about **guarding the vault**. It's the technical and organizational measures to keep data safe from unauthorized access, loss, or destruction.
*   **Focus**: Security, Integrity, Availability.
*   **Example**: Encryption, Firewalls, Backups, Access Logs.
*   **Bob's View**: "My database is unhackable."

### 2. Data Privacy
This is about **the user's rights**. It's about who is allowed to collect data, why, and how they use it. It's the right to be left alone and to control one's own information.
*   **Focus**: Rights, Consent, Transparency, Fairness.
*   **Example**: Asking for permission, collecting only what is needed, deleting data when asked.
*   **Alice's View**: "It doesn't matter if the vault is unhackable if you shouldn't have put my secrets in there in the first place."
