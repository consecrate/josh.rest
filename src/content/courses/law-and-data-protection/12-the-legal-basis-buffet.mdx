# The Menu

After the "Email List Fiasco," Bob is cautious. He wants to process data correctly.
"So I need to ask for permission for *everything*?" he asks.

Alice shakes her head. "No. That's a common myth. 'Consent' is just one item on the menu. There are **6 Lawful Bases** in Article 6. You must choose the right one for the job."

Think of it like a buffet. You can't take everything; you have to pick the one plate that fits your hunger (purpose).

## The 6 Lawful Bases

### 1. Contract (The "Business Deal")
*   **When to use**: You need the data to fulfill a contract with the user, or to take steps at their request before entering a contract.
*   **Example**: Delivering a package, processing a credit card payment, paying an employee.
*   **Why**: If they don't give you the address, you can't ship the item. It's necessary.

### 2. Legal Obligation (The "Law Made Me Do It")
*   **When to use**: You are required by *another* law (not GDPR) to process data.
*   **Example**: Sending salary data to the tax office, performing KYC (Know Your Customer) checks for banking, keeping invoices for tax audits.
*   **Note**: This must be a statutory obligation, not just a contractual one.

### 3. Vital Interests (The "Life Saver")
*   **When to use**: To protect someone's life.
*   **Example**: An unconscious car crash victim arrives at the ER. The hospital searches their ID for allergies.
*   **Limit**: Only for life-or-death situations where consent is impossible.

### 4. Public Task (The "Official Duty")
*   **When to use**: You are a public authority (or acting as one) performing an official function.
*   **Example**: Tax collection, garbage collection, police work, public health administration.
*   **Who**: Mostly government agencies.

### 5. Legitimate Interests (The "Balancing Act")
*   **When to use**: You have a valid interest (commercial, security, etc.) *AND* it doesn't override the user's rights.
*   **Example**: Fraud detection, network security, direct marketing (sometimes), personalized content.
*   **Requirement**: You must do a "Balancing Test" (LIA). Does your benefit outweigh their privacy risk?

### 6. Consent (The "Permission Slip")
*   **When to use**: When none of the above apply, and you want to give the user a genuine choice.
*   **Example**: Sending a newsletter, tracking cookies, sharing data with third parties.
*   **Warning**: Consent is hard to get right (see next lesson). Don't use it if you don't need to!
